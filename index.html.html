Creative Direction
Content Themes & Tone
Hazheart's brand has range — playful, aggressive, and provocative. End Overdose content should live in that same space. Life-saving messages don't have to be sterile.
Theme 01
Suggestive & Fun
PSA content that makes people stop scrolling — personality-forward and unafraid to use Hazheart's existing aesthetic to pull people in before delivering the message.
Lil Aaron PSA Social clips In-store content
Theme 02
Aggressive & Edgy
The Flame Guy energy — direct and in-your-face. Content that treats the overdose crisis with the urgency it deserves and doesn't soften the message for comfort. Can be fun and use people who are socially relevant.
End Overdose Live Festival booth PSA openers
Theme 03
Playful & Earnest
The Narcat lane — warm, gamified, genuinely fun. Content that sneaks education into entertainment. Narcats World, animated intros, in-store kiosk moments. No preachiness, just vibes and life-saving info — think Towelie but opioids.
Narcats World Claw machine Animated PSA intros
The guiding principle
Good content doesn't have to choose between entertaining and life-saving. Hazheart's audience responds to authenticity and edge — not sanitized public health messaging. Every piece we make together should feel like it belongs in the Hazheart universe first, and carry the End Overdose message naturally.
The Vision
Why Hazheart?
Traditional public health campaigns miss the people most at risk. Hazheart has built real trust with a real audience. That trust is the rarest resource in harm reduction work.
🎯
Reach the unreachable
People who tune out government PSAs listen to the brands and artists they love. Hazheart's community already shows up — now we give them something that could save a life.
📡
Infrastructure already built
hazheart.tv, the retail store, a loyal social following, festival presence — the distribution network exists. We bring the mission, the training expertise, and the content.
🏪
Physical + digital
In-store game kiosks, a naloxone claw machine, PSA content on shop screens, regular co-branded social posts, and multi-format video clipping across every channel.
🏅
Civic credibility
Document the impact, then pursue official recognition from LA City Council, the California State Legislature, and federal Congress — turning culture into institutional legacy.

Public-Facing Outreach

What we're going after
A City of Los Angeles Certificate of Recognition — signed by the Mayor — awarded to End Overdose and Hazheart for community overdose prevention leadership in Van Nuys and the greater LA area. The same recognition given to organizations like Art Hearts Fashion for their decade of contributions to the city.
Why it matters
Official civic recognition transforms Hazheart from a cultural brand into a documented community institution. It unlocks grant eligibility, strengthens media narratives, and gives the collaboration permanent, framed proof of impact.
The path to getting it
Document a year of community impact → approach Councilmember Imelda Padilla (District 6, Van Nuys) → request the formal commendation. Certificates flow from the council district office up to the Mayor's signature.
